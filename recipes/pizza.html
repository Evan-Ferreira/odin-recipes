Pizza

Description

a culinary dish made with a flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, vegetables, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Your choice of toppings (pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, etc.)

Steps

  1. Gather all your ingredients.
  2. In a large bowl, dissolve sugar in warm water. Sprinkle yeast over the top, and let stand for about 10 minutes, until foamy.
  3. Stir in flour, salt, and olive oil. Beat until smooth. Let rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and pat or roll into a round. Transfer crust to a lightly greased pizza pan or baker's peel dusted with cornmeal.
  5. Spread pizza sauce over the crust. Top with cheese and your favorite toppings.
  6. Bake in preheated oven at 475 degrees F (245 degrees C) for about 15 to 20 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and melted.
